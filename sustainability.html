SUSTAINABILITY

Clear on our responsibility.

Clear packaging and environmental responsibility aren't opposites. Here's how DCP approaches sustainability — through recyclable materials, responsible sourcing, and food-safe standards.

OUR APPROACH

Three sustainability pillars.

Recyclable Materials

The majority of our clear containers are made from RPET (recycled PET) — one of the most widely accepted recyclable plastics in the U.S. Containers can re-enter the materials stream after use, reducing pressure on virgin plastic.

Food-Safe Standards

Every product in our catalog meets FDA food-contact material standards. No compromise on food safety — ever. The same material transparency we offer in our containers, we apply to sourcing.

Responsible Sourcing

We work with factory partners who meet quality and compliance standards at the production level — not just at the label. Our sourcing process is intentional, not just transactional.

WHY RPET

Clear, food-safe, recyclable.

RPET (recycled polyethylene terephthalate) is the workhorse of clear packaging — and one of the few plastics with established curbside recycling infrastructure across the United States.

The same crystal clarity that lets shoppers see your product is what allows RPET to be sorted, washed, and re-melted into new containers. It's a closed loop — when consumers do their part.

ALTERNATIVE MATERIALS

Beyond standard RPET.

When your product or brand requires a different material, we source it. Our supply chain isn't locked to a single plastic.

PLA (Plant-Based)

Polylactic acid — derived from corn or sugarcane. Compostable in industrial facilities. Best for brands marketing actively to sustainability-conscious shoppers.

PP (Polypropylene)

Higher heat tolerance than RPET. Used for hot-fill and microwave-safe applications. Widely accepted in #5 recycling streams.

100% Recycled Content

Higher post-consumer recycled content blends available on request. Talk to us about your sustainability claims and the recycled content thresholds to match.

OPERATIONAL

Efficiency at every step.

Ocean container freight is the most carbon-efficient mode for high-volume shipments — significantly lower per-unit emissions than air freight. Our supply chain leans on it by default.

U.S. warehouses positioned for regional fulfillment reduce last-mile transit. Recurring orders are planned to minimize partial-load shipments. Efficiency isn't just commercial — it's environmental.
